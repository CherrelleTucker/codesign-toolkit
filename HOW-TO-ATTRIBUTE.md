## *Ways to Show Appreciation and Help Us Track Success*

---

> **Mutual Success**: When you use this toolkit and find it valuable, there are several ways to give credit that help us demonstrate impact and improve the resources for everyone.

---

## üéØ Why Attribution Matters

### **For You:**
- **Credibility**: Shows you're using evidence-based, NASA-validated methodologies
- **Transparency**: Demonstrates professional standards in your work
- **Community**: Connects you with other practitioners using similar approaches
- **Support**: Helps us know you're a user if you need help or have questions

### **For Us:**
- **Impact Measurement**: Helps demonstrate toolkit effectiveness to NASA leadership
- **Resource Justification**: Shows value to continue developing and maintaining tools
- **Community Building**: Identifies practitioners for knowledge sharing and collaboration
- **Improvement**: Feedback helps us enhance tools and create new ones

### **For the Community:**
- **Visibility**: Raises awareness of co-design practices in Earth observation
- **Validation**: Real-world usage proves methodologies work across contexts
- **Learning**: Creates opportunities to share lessons learned and best practices

### **üîó Link and Reference (Easy)**

**In Project Documentation:**

- Tag @NASA or relevant NASA accounts when sharing successes
- Use hashtags: #CoDesign #EarthObservation #NASA #UserCenteredDesign
- Share specific tool URLs that helped your project

### **üìä Share Your Success Story (Valuable)**

**GitHub Discussions:**
Post in our [GitHub Discussions](https://github.com/your-repo/discussions) about:
- Which tools you used and how you adapted them
- What worked well and what was challenging
- Results achieved and lessons learned
- Recommendations for other practitioners

**Lessons Learned:** [What would you do differently]
**Recommendation:** [Would you recommend to others? Why?]

**Annual Reports/Publications:**
- Include toolkit usage in annual accomplishment reports
- Mention in project success stories and case studies
- Reference in proposals as evidence of systematic approach

### **Usage Metrics We Track**

**Repository Analytics:**
- GitHub stars, forks, and watchers
- Issue views and engagement
- Discussion participation and community growth
- Geographic distribution of users

**Tool Adoption:**
- Which tools are most/least used
- Download patterns and seasonal trends
- User pathways through different tools
- Community-contributed improvements

### **Impact Indicators We Value**

**Project Outcomes:**
- Number of Earth observation solutions developed using toolkit methods
- Improvement in stakeholder satisfaction and engagement
- Reduction in development time or rework cycles
- Evidence of sustained solution adoption

**Community Growth:**
- Citations in academic literature and reports
- Mentions in conference presentations and workshops
- Adaptation for new domains or contexts
- Training programs incorporating toolkit methods

**Institutional Adoption:**
- NASA centers and programs using toolkit systematically
- Other agencies or organizations adopting methods
- Integration into official processes and requirements
- Scaling from individual projects to program-wide use

### **Success Stories We Love to Hear**

**High-Impact Examples:**
- "Using stakeholder mapping, we identified a key user group we'd missed, leading to 40% higher adoption"
- "Co-design workshops prevented major rework that would have cost 6 months"
- "The requirements canvas helped bridge communication gaps between our team and users"

**Unexpected Applications:**
- Tools adapted for non-Earth observation domains
- International collaborations using SERVIR-derived methods
- Student projects and educational applications
- Cross-agency or intergovernmental initiatives

**Community Building:**
- "We trained 20 staff on these methods and now use them organization-wide"
- "Connected with other practitioners through the GitHub discussions"
- "Presented our toolkit-based approach at [conference], inspiring others"

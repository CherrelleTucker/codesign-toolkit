### **NASA NSITE (NASA Satellite Needs Working Group Implementation Team)**

**Program Overview:**
The NASA Satellite Needs Working Group Implementation Team (NSITE) develops and implements solutions to address Earth observation needs identified by U.S. government agencies.

**Contributed Materials:**
- **NSITE Solution Project Plan Template** - Core co-design definitions, stakeholder engagement frameworks, touchpoint planning
- **NSITE Solution Project Requirements and Expectations** - Implementation requirements, collaborative approach specifications, training development
- **NSITE SEP and Co-Design Plan, Vision, Structure** - Program-level strategy, touchpoint framework, sustainability planning
- **SNWG Discovery Interview Blueprint** - Structured user research methodology
- **Solution Implementation Workflow Checklist DRAFT** - Operational checklists, validation procedures
- **NSITE Solution Brainstorming & Stakeholder Mapping Miro Templates** - Workshop templates and facilitation materials

**Key Contributions to This Toolkit:**
- Official NASA definition of User-Centered Design and co-design
- Systematic touchpoint framework for stakeholder engagement
- Requirements for collaborative solution development
- Stakeholder engagement lead role definition and expectations

**Contact/Access:** NSITE Program Office, NASA Goddard Space Flight Center

### **SERVIR (NASA-USAID Joint Program)**

**Program Overview:**
SERVIR is a joint initiative of NASA and the United States Agency for International Development (USAID) that works with countries around the world to address critical challenges in food security, water resources, weather and climate, land use, and natural disasters using Earth observation data.

**Contributed Materials:**
- **SERVIR Service Planning Toolkit - 2021 Revision** - Comprehensive service design methodology
- **Service Design Tool - SERVIR Service Planning Toolkit 2021** - Workshop-based collaborative planning approach
- **Stakeholder Mapping Tool - SERVIR Service Planning Toolkit 2021** - Four-step participatory stakeholder analysis
- **Capacity Building and Training Definition Document** - User enablement and sustainability strategies
- **Data Management Definition Document (DMDD)** - Technical integration and governance frameworks
- **Information Chain Analysis Overview** - Information flow mapping methodology

**Key Contributions to This Toolkit:**
- Proven methodologies for Earth observation service development
- International perspective on co-design with diverse partners
- Systematic stakeholder mapping and analysis processes
- Capacity building and training frameworks
- Service concept development and impact pathway planning

**Access:** Available through SERVIR regional hubs and NASA Applied Sciences Division
**Website:** [servir.adpc.net](https://servir.adpc.net) | [nasa.gov/servir](https://nasa.gov/servir)

### **MSFC (Marshall Space Flight Center) Solution Co-Development Initiative**

**Program Overview:**
Marshall Space Flight Center's Applied Sciences Division leads initiatives to systematically implement co-design processes across NASA Earth science solution development.

**Contributed Materials:**
- **MSFC Coordination on Solutions Co-Development Toolkit** - Organizational implementation guidance, phase-based workflows
- **Solution Co-Development Toolkit Narrative** - Process philosophy, detailed workflows, iterative development approaches
- **Meeting Notes - Technical Development CoDesign Toolkit Working Group 2025** - Practical lessons learned, tool refinement processes
- **Bi-Weekly Coordination Meetings MSFC Co-Development Toolkit** - Collaborative planning approaches, cross-team coordination

**Key Contributions to This Toolkit:**
- Organizational change management for co-design implementation
- Phase-based workflow structures and decision frameworks
- Technical team integration with user-centered processes  
- Systematic approach to scaling co-design across programs

**Contact:** Applied Sciences Division, Marshall Space Flight Center

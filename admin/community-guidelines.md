### **Issue Triage and Management**

<details>
<summary><strong>üìã Systematic Issue Processing</strong></summary>

#### **Daily Triage Process** (15-30 minutes each morning)

**Step 1: New Issue Assessment**
- [ ] **Read Completely**: Understand the full context and request
- [ ] **Categorize Appropriately**: Apply correct labels (category, difficulty, phase)
- [ ] **Assess Priority**: Determine urgency based on impact and user need
- [ ] **Identify Expertise Needed**: Determine if specialized knowledge required
- [ ] **Assign Appropriately**: Route to right maintainer if specialized

**Step 2: Response Planning**
- [ ] **Immediate Response**: Can this be answered right now?
- [ ] **Research Required**: What information/investigation is needed?
- [ ] **Multiple Steps**: Does this require a process or multiple actions?
- [ ] **Community Input**: Would community discussion be valuable?
- [ ] **Timeline Estimation**: How long will full resolution take?

**Step 3: Action Implementation**
- [ ] **Quick Responses**: Handle simple questions immediately
- [ ] **Research Tasks**: Gather needed information for complex issues
- [ ] **Community Facilitation**: Encourage relevant community discussion
- [ ] **Expert Consultation**: Involve specialists for technical questions
- [ ] **Process Initiation**: Begin multi-step resolution processes

#### **Issue Classification System**

**Priority Levels:**
- **üî¥ Critical**: Toolkit-breaking bugs, harmful guidance, urgent stakeholder needs
- **üü° High**: User-blocking issues, popular feature requests, community-requested improvements
- **üü¢ Medium**: Enhancement suggestions, documentation improvements, optimization opportunities
- **‚ö™ Low**: Nice-to-have features, minor improvements, future considerations

**Complexity Levels:**
- **üå± Simple**: Can be resolved by any maintainer in < 30 minutes
- **üìà Moderate**: Requires specific knowledge or research, 1-4 hours resolution
- **üéØ Complex**: Requires multiple experts, significant research, or community input

### **Conflict Prevention Strategies**

<details>
<summary><strong>üõ°Ô∏è Proactive Community Health Management</strong></summary>

#### **Early Warning Signs**
- **Tone Deterioration**: Increasing negative or frustrated communication
- **Repeat Disagreements**: Same individuals consistently in conflict
- **Process Frustration**: Complaints about toolkit processes or decisions
- **Expertise Challenges**: Questioning of maintainer knowledge or authority
- **Community Fragmentation**: Formation of separate discussion groups

#### **Prevention Techniques**
- **Clear Expectations**: Regularly reinforce community guidelines and standards
- **Transparent Processes**: Explain decisions and reasoning publicly
- **Proactive Communication**: Address concerns before they become conflicts
- **Individual Outreach**: Private conversations with frustrated community members
- **Process Improvement**: Adapt procedures based on community feedback

#### **Relationship Building Activities**
- **Success Recognition**: Celebrate community contributions publicly
- **Expert Spotlighting**: Highlight community members' expertise and achievements
- **Collaborative Projects**: Create opportunities for joint problem-solving
- **Learning Sharing**: Facilitate knowledge exchange between community members
- **Informal Interaction**: Encourage casual conversation and relationship building

</details>

### **Conflict Resolution Process**

<details>
<summary><strong>üîß Structured Conflict Management</strong></summary>

#### **Level 1: Direct Resolution** (Maintainer handles directly)

**When to Use**: Minor disagreements, process confusion, simple misunderstandings

**Resolution Steps:**
1. **Acknowledge All Perspectives**: "I can see that both [Person A] and [Person B] have valid concerns about..."
2. **Clarify the Issue**: "Let me make sure I understand the core disagreement..."
3. **Provide Framework**: Reference community guidelines, toolkit principles, or evidence
4. **Facilitate Agreement**: "Given our community standards, would you both be comfortable with..."
5. **Follow Up**: Check back to ensure resolution is working

**Resolution Template:**
```markdown
Hi [names],

I can see we have different perspectives on [issue]. This kind of discussion is valuable for our community when we can work through it constructively.

Let me share some context that might help:
[Relevant background, guidelines, or evidence]

Based on our community standards around [relevant principle], I'd suggest we move forward with [proposed resolution] because [rationale].

[Person A], does this address your concern about [specific issue]?
[Person B], does this approach work for your situation with [specific context]?

I'm happy to discuss this further if needed. Our goal is finding solutions that serve the community well.
```

#### **Level 2: Mediated Resolution** (Multiple maintainers involved)

**When to Use**: Ongoing disagreements, multiple community members involved, process or policy disputes

**Resolution Process:**
1. **Assessment**: Multiple maintainers review situation and perspectives
2. **Strategy Development**: Agree on approach and lead mediator
3. **Individual Conversations**: Private discussions with each party
4. **Mediated Discussion**: Facilitated public or private conversation
5. **Resolution Agreement**: Clear agreement on path forward
6. **Community Communication**: Transparent update on resolution (if public issue)

#### **Level 3: Administrative Resolution** (Senior leadership involvement)

**When to Use**: Serious community guideline violations, harassment, repeated disruptive behavior

**Escalation Triggers:**
- Personal attacks or harassment
- Repeated refusal to follow community guidelines
- Behavior that threatens community safety or inclusivity
- Conflicts involving maintainer conduct

**Resolution Options:**
- **Warning**: Formal notification of guideline violation
- **Temporary Restrictions**: Limited participation while resolution in progress
- **Mediated Agreement**: Formal agreement on future behavior
- **Community Removal**: Permanent removal from community (extreme cases only)

</details>

## üö® Crisis Management and Emergency Response

### **Community Crisis Scenarios**

<details>
<summary><strong>‚ö†Ô∏è Emergency Response Protocols</strong></summary>

#### **Crisis Type Classification**

**Level 1: Minor Disruption** (Single maintainer response)
- Individual user frustration or confusion
- Minor process disagreement
- Temporary increase in support needs
- Small-scale misinformation

**Level 2: Moderate Crisis** (Team coordination required)
- Multiple users experiencing similar problems
- Maintainer availability issues during high-need period
- Significant process or policy disagreement
- Quality issues affecting multiple tools

**Level 3: Major Crisis** (Leadership involvement required)
- Harassment or serious community guideline violations
- Organized criticism or attack on community
- Major security or safety issues
- Significant stakeholder concerns about community management

#### **Crisis Response Framework**

**Immediate Response (Within 2 hours):**
1. **Assess Situation**: Understand scope, impact, and urgency
2. **Ensure Safety**: Address any immediate safety or harassment issues
3. **Coordinate Team**: Alert appropriate maintainers and leadership
4. **Initial Communication**: Acknowledge issue and indicate response timeline
5. **Document Everything**: Record all relevant information and communications

**Short-term Response (Within 24 hours):**
1. **Detailed Investigation**: Gather complete information and context
2. **Solution Development**: Create appropriate response plan
3. **Stakeholder Communication**: Update relevant parties on status and plans
4. **Community Communication**: Transparent update to community about situation
5. **Implementation**: Begin executing solution plan

**Long-term Follow-up (Within 1 week):**
1. **Resolution Verification**: Ensure crisis is fully resolved
2. **Community Healing**: Address any ongoing community relationship issues
3. **Process Improvement**: Update procedures to prevent similar crises
4. **Documentation**: Create case study for future reference
5. **Relationship Rebuilding**: Repair any damaged community relationships

#### **Crisis Communication Templates**

**Immediate Acknowledgment:**
```markdown

- [Next communication timeline]
```
